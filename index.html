The World After Corona: Is Business Travel Back?

New research out from Travel Leaders Group, which now owns Coronas, Ovation and Motto, indicates that business travel is recovering and signs of growth in the hospitality and leisure sectors are evident.

“Despite the economic slowdown, travel levels remain steady. We’re seeing a steady number of meetings and large group bookings at more than 3,000 hotels, and a continued increase in small group bookings,” said Bob Marcotte, president of Travel Leaders Group, Americas. “Our research indicates there are indications that travel is rebounding, and that millennials and business travel are leading the rebound.”

Coronas found that more than 90 percent of travel decision-makers have reported an increase in bookings from January to June of 2016 versus the same period in 2015. And more than half (55 percent) of respondents said they were planning to travel abroad this year, an increase of more than 10 percentage points over last year.
